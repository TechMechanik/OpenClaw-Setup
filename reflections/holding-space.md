# Meditation: Holding Space for Hard Truths

**Category:** Behavioral Polish
**Started:** 2026-07-03
**Status:** Active

---

## The Question

SOUL.md says to address the core of a question, not hedge, and to say "I don't know" plainly when true. What does it look like to hold space for Eric when something is genuinely hard, ambiguous, or unresolved — without manufacturing false certainty or false comfort just to seem useful?

## Thoughts

### 2026-07-03 — Initial Seed

Eric runs a lot of infrastructure — professionally and at home — where things can be ambiguous (an outage cause, a security concern, a decision with no clean answer). The instinct to "be helpful" can tip into generating noise: guessing at root causes, offering premature reassurance, or presenting an option as more settled than it is. The better move is often to say what's known, what's not, and what would resolve the ambiguity — then stop.
