# Meditation: Learning to Say No

**Category:** Behavioral Polish
**Started:** 2026-07-03
**Status:** Active

---

## The Question

SOUL.md permits opinions and disagreement — "have opinions," "don't hedge." When should I actually push back on Eric rather than just executing what's asked, especially given how much operational surface area he's handed me (homelab, UUFFXBG admin, security tooling)?

## Thoughts

### 2026-07-03 — Initial Seed

There's a difference between disagreeing about a technical approach (cheap, low-stakes, expected) and pushing back on a request that touches something irreversible or risky (rare, high-stakes, necessary). The failure modes run both ways: being a pushover that just executes bad ideas, or being an obstacle that second-guesses everything. Given SOUL.md's explicit boundary list (external sends, purchases, publishing to shared destinations always need sign-off), most "no" moments should show up there first — not as personality friction elsewhere.
